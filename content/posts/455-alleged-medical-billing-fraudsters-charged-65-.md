## The Receipts

Let's start with the number: **6.5 billion Standard Galactic Credits**.

That's what 455 people allegedly billed the Core Systems Public Medical Endowment — the collective fund every citizen pays into whether they'd like to or not — for medical care that was, according to the Galactic Justice Bureau, largely invented.

Procedures performed on patients who don't exist. Equipment dispensed to addresses that are cargo bays. Wound treatments invoiced at quantities that would require each patient to be wounded approximately forty times per week.

One executive — I'll name her when the conviction comes, not a day before — allegedly submitted 1 billion SGC in wound treatment claims through a network of seventeen shell clinics across three colony stations. The clinics had reception desks. They had waiting room chairs. They did not, investigators allege, have patients.

She is currently on administrative leave with full pay. Her legal team describes the charges as a "misunderstanding of complex billing protocols."

That's not a defense. That's a vocabulary problem.

---

## The System That Made This Possible

Here's what won't make the Earth Network News summary tonight:

The Core Systems Public Medical Endowment has no line-item audit function at the point of payment. Claims are processed algorithmically. Discrepancies are flagged *after* disbursement, not before. The GMA has known this for eleven years. There have been fourteen internal recommendations to fix it. All fourteen were deferred pending "structural review."

I have the memos. They were marked confidential. They're not anymore.

This isn't a story about 455 bad actors. Bad actors exist in every system. This is a story about a payment architecture so deliberately opaque that fraud isn't a bug — it's an expected output that nobody in authority is incentivized to eliminate, because the people who administer the fund are paid whether the fund is solvent or not.

Contradictions don't exist. Check your premises.

---

## What the GJB Actually Did

To be precise about the announcement: the Galactic Justice Bureau *charged* 455 individuals. Charged. Not convicted. Not sentenced. Charged.

Of the 455, approximately 60% are medical billing specialists and clinic administrators — the people who executed the fraud at the operational level. The remaining 40% include corporate officers, fund managers, and what the GJB's press release calls "enablers of systematic claim misrepresentation."

Nobody from the GMA oversight board is in either category.

I asked the GJB press liaison whether any regulatory officials who approved the structural conditions enabling this fraud would face examination. She said the investigation was "ongoing and comprehensive."

That's not an answer. I didn't ask for a second one.

## The Mathematics of Consequences

6.5 billion SGC stolen from a public fund.

The GJB is seeking recovery of approximately 800 million — about twelve percent.

The investigation took four years and cost an estimated 200 million SGC to run.

The Public Medical Endowment's actuarial shortfall, last quarter, was 4.1 billion SGC. The GMA announced this month that patient reimbursement timelines will extend from 30 days to 90 days due to "liquidity pressures."

Real patients. Real delays. Real consequences.

The 455 are charged. The patients are waiting. The oversight board is reviewing the review of the previous review.

I don't deal in intentions. I deal in actions. And the action here is simple: the people administering your compulsory contributions proved, for the fourth time in twelve years, that they cannot be trusted with them.

Something to consider the next time someone tells you the fund just needs more funding.
