No GCB registry number. No Interstellar Assembly manifest. No Colonial Administration tax code, no Standard Galactic Credit subsidy application, no approved fabrication quota, no licensed medical bureau inspector.\n\nNothing.\n\nAnd yet — *and here's where it gets interesting* — independent researchers who've actually been there describe a place with clean water reclamators, functioning gravity plating on every level, food markets stocked with actual variety, and — I want you to sit with this one — **zero bureaucratic debt**.\n\nThe place is called Station Galt.\n\nI'll give you a moment.\n\n---\n\n## HERE'S THE WHITEBOARD MOMENT\n\nEvery registered colony in the Core Systems follows the same lifecycle. No committee.\n\nThey found a rock in an unregistered debris field, anchored to it, and *built what they actually needed.*\n\nTheir head architect, known only by the callsign **ROARK**, reportedly rejected every external design committee that tried to attach itself to the project. Pay attention, because this is where the scam becomes visible.\n\nEvery registered colony receives GCB "development credits" — freshly diluted, printed from nothing — to fund approved construction. The GCB creates the credits. The credits flow first to the contractors on the approved vendor list. The contractors are connected to the mega-corps. *The mega-corps sit on the Colonial Development Advisory Council that approves the vendor list.*\n\n**AND THAT'S THE CANTILLON EFFECT, BABY.**\n\nThe credits arrive at the colony last, already diluted, already devalued by the time they've passed through six layers of connected hands. The colony gets the debt. The advisory council gets the spread.\n\nStation Galt received none of those credits. Which means it also received none of that debt.\n\nThey traded directly. Fabricated what they needed. Designed structures for *their* environment instead of Assembly Standard Form ergonomics designed for a committee member's office on Earth Station.\n\n---\n\n## THE REACTION\n\nWhen ENN ran a single paragraph about Galt last year — buried in a Colonial Development Index footnote — the Colonial Administration's response was immediate:\n\n*"Unregistered settlements pose structural safety risks and cannot be verified for habitability compliance."*\n\nThe habitability compliance. Of the colony with the highest reported standard of living in the sector.\n\nI want you to notice what they didn't say. They didn't say it was poor. They didn't say people were suffering. They said it wasn't *verified.*\n\nBecause a thriving colony that nobody approved is the most dangerous thing in the galaxy to people who sell approvals for a living.\n\n---\n\n## THE COORDINATES\n\nI don't have them. I asked. Multiple sources went quiet.\n\nBut here's what I *do* know: at least three other unregistered settlements have contacted Station Galt requesting what they're calling — and I love this — *"the fabrication philosophy."*\n\nNot the blueprints. Not the schematics.\n\n**The philosophy.**\n\nBuild what you need. Sign your name to it. Owe no one for the right to exist.\n\nSomeone's going to find those coordinates eventually. When they do, I suspect the Colonial Administration is going to need a much bigger committee.\n\n---\n\n*ヴィクター 清水 is the Economics & Trade Editor for Cassette Future Magazine.
