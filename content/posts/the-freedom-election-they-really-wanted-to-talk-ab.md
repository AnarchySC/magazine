## The Freedom Election: They Really Wanted to Talk About Collectivism

The Interstellar Assembly's Consolidated Charter Bloc has finalized its platform for the Cycle 2936 Assembly elections. It's in. It's official. They workshopped it.

The platform is: freedom versus collectivism.

Not standard galactic credit dilution, which is at a 40-year high. Not the Proxima supply chain collapse, which has made basic protein fabrication unaffordable for roughly 800 million Frontier settlers. Not the Solar Defense Compact's ongoing undeclared engagement in the Kepler Corridor, which the Assembly technically hasn't voted on, hasn't funded, and cannot legally explain. Not the housing access crisis on orbital stations, where median rent now consumes 71% of a standard worker's monthly credit allocation.

Collectivism. That's the one.

"The election," declared Assembly Floor Director Harlan Voss at a press gathering on Tuesday, "will be about whether we maintain our freedom under the Charter, or surrender to the collectivist forces that seek to centralize all galactic resources under a single authority."

He said this. In the Interstellar Assembly building. Which is a centralized authority. That controls galactic resources.

And nobody laughed?

Wait, it gets better.

The Consolidated Charter Bloc's platform document — 340 pages, available on the Assembly neural feed if you enjoy punishment — uses the word "freedom" 847 times. It uses the word "fabrication costs" zero times. "Credit dilution" appears once, in a footnote, attributed to the *opposing* bloc as evidence of collectivist sympathies.

I'm not saying it's a grift. I'm just reading their platform aloud.

The Outer Rim Coalition, naturally, is the villain in this story. Coalition settlements have been experimenting with cooperative resource-sharing models, which the Bloc's document describes as "proto-collectivist infrastructure threatening the voluntary order of the Core Systems." The Coalition settlements adopted cooperative models, incidentally, because the GCB's prime lending rate made independent credit access functionally impossible for anyone outside the Core. The Bloc also voted for that rate. Twice.

They said this. Then they did this. I'll wait.

The irony worth savoring — and I am savoring it, slowly, like a man who has nowhere to be — is that the Charter Bloc's platform offers exactly one concrete economic policy: a 12% reduction in Assembly oversight fees for registered megacorp fabrication operations. Which would be paid for by reallocating Frontier Settlement development credits.

So: less resource access for frontier settlers, more for centralized corporate entities, and the campaign message is *freedom from centralization*.

Voss, to his credit, gave a 40-minute speech about this without his face doing anything unusual. That's professional. That's a skill.

The Bloc's internal polling reportedly shows that "collectivism" outperforms "economic policy" as a voter mobilization frame by 23 points among Core Systems registered voters. Which tells you something about Core Systems registered voters, or about polling, or about what happens when credit dilution eats your savings but the enemy has a scarier name.

Anyway.

The Cycle 2936 elections are in eight months. The galactic credit is at its lowest purchasing power since the Outer Rim Secession Accords. Orbital station housing waitlists are running 4-to-11 years. The undeclared Kepler engagement has now cost more in fleet personnel and materiel than the Assembly's entire infrastructure appropriation for the decade.

But the election will be about collectivism.

I just think it's funny.
