# The Medical Bill Nobody Reads at the Zero-G Arena
### 観客は熱狂する。負傷した選手は請求書を受け取る。

---

The Callisto Drift League opened its 2935 season last week with a promotional burst across every neural feed in the Core Systems: *「重力なし。限界なし。」* No gravity. No limits.

The manifest doesn't match the cargo.

I pulled the league's own injury disclosure filings, which are public record under the Frontier Athletic Transparency Act — a law, incidentally, that the league spent 4.2 million SGC lobbying against before it passed. Season 2934: 847 registered competitors. 312 injury incidents. 61 classified as "career-altering." Eleven fatalities, listed in the filing under the category *"extraordinary competitive outcomes."*

Extraordinary competitive outcomes.

The CDL's standard competitor contract runs 340 pages. I read it. The medical liability clause sits on page 218, subsection 7(c)(iv), rendered in a font size that would challenge a neural-implant optician. The league covers acute trauma care for injuries occurring "within the defined competition boundary" during "sanctioned event windows." Everything else — rehabilitation, chronic joint degradation, the neurological sequencing disorders that show up eighteen months after a hard impact in vacuum — that's the athlete's problem.

The average CDL competitor earns 47,000 SGC per season. A full course of post-career neuro-rehabilitation at a Core Systems facility runs between 80,000 and 340,000 SGC, depending on severity. The Frontier settlements where most CDL athletes originate don't have Core Systems facilities. They have what they have.

Free healthcare for athletes, they said at the league's founding press event in 2921. I checked the fine print. The word "free" does not appear anywhere in 340 pages.

---

I spoke to Taira Yusuf, a drift-class competitor who spent four seasons in the CDL's mid-tier circuit before a vascular decompression event ended his career in 2932. He lives on Ganymede Station now, two decks below the habitat's commercial ring, which means he is paying 2,300 SGC monthly for atmospheric quality that requires a filter unit to be tolerable.

"They put my face on a promotional banner for two years," he said. "Didn't ask. Didn't pay. Just used it."

The CDL earns approximately 2.3 billion SGC annually in broadcast licensing fees to ENN Sports Division and regional neural-feed aggregators. Franchise valuations run between 400 million and 1.1 billion SGC per team. Callisto Drift Incorporated, the parent holding structure, reported 340 million SGC in net earnings last quarter.

Nobody ever asks what it costs to enforce a 340-page contract against a twenty-four-year-old from a frontier settlement who has never hired a lawyer.

---

The Interstellar Athletic Bureau issued "voluntary wellness guidelines" for zero-G competition in 2929. Voluntary. The CDL acknowledged receipt of the guidelines. There is no record of implementation.

The Solar Defense Compact's Fleet Athletic Program, which runs parallel zero-G training circuits for personnel, reports injury rates 34% lower than civilian league averages. They achieve this by limiting deceleration-impact thresholds and mandating quarterly neurological screening. The CDL considered adopting similar protocols in 2931. The working group estimated a 12% reduction in "peak entertainment value" based on viewer engagement metrics.

They tabled the proposal.

Taira Yusuf's filter unit needs replacing. The replacement costs 800 SGC. He's been managing the degraded air for about six weeks now while he figures out the freight.

Somewhere on Callisto, an executive is reviewing Q3 projections.

I wonder which of them will still be watching the season finale in twenty years.
