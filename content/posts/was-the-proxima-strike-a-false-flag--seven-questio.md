# Was the Proxima Strike a False Flag? — Seven Questions Nobody Will Answer

*by 宗像 レイナ, Galactic Affairs Correspondent*

---

It has been three years.

Three years since the Proxima Centauri Colonial Corridor absorbed the most catastrophically coordinated surprise strike in the history of the Core Systems. Thousands dead inside a single standard rotation. Outer Rim Coalition cells breached six separate defense perimeters simultaneously. The Solar Defense Compact's forward listening network — the one consuming 4.7 billion SGC annually to prevent exactly this — detected nothing.

The official story has not changed. It went like this: intelligence failure, systemic breakdown, tragic oversight. The Interstellar Assembly convened three separate inquiry committees. All three concluded the same thing. Nobody was held responsible. Several of the presiding officers received commendations.

I don't deal in intentions. I deal in actions. And the actions surrounding that day do not fit the story we were sold.

So here are seven questions. I asked them to four separate Assembly spokespeople over the past month. I am still waiting.

---

**Question One: Why was the SDC's Proxima Early Warning Array powered to thirty percent capacity on that specific rotation?**

Maintenance logs obtained through the Independent Transmission Network — not ENN, who never asked — confirm the drawdown began sixteen hours before the strike. The authorized override carried the digital signature of a Terran Intelligence Bureau liaison whose name is redacted in all public filings. That's not an answer. Try again.

**Question Two: Who gave the stand-down order to the Corridor's rapid-response fleet?**

Flight traffic records show three interceptor squadrons queued for launch, held at bay for ninety-one minutes while the strike unfolded. Ninety-one minutes. A command authorization code was used. That code belonged to an officer who died in a shuttle accident six weeks prior. Nobody in the Assembly inquiry thought this detail was relevant enough to question on the record.

**Question Three: Which Mega-Corp had active short positions on Proxima Colonial Infrastructure bonds the week before?**

Ceres Exchange settlement data is public. I looked. Stellar Financial Holdings moved a position worth approximately 340 million SGC into inverse infrastructure instruments on Proxima-linked assets five days before the attack. They closed the position thirty hours after the strike for a return of 2.1 billion SGC. The Assembly's financial subcommittee called this 'coincidental market activity.' You said the opposite about Outer Rim traders in 2931. Which version is lying?

**Question Four: Where was the TIB's Proxima Station chief during the strike window?**

This is a simple factual question. The TIB has refused to answer it for three years. ENN has not asked it once in three years. I have asked it eleven times. The silence is its own kind of answer.

**Question Five: Why were the three surviving SDC commanders rotated out of service within ninety days — quietly, with full pension, no disciplinary notation?**

You don't reward failures that spectacularly. You don't quietly pension three officers without a single reprimand in the file unless you need them not talking. Don't tell me what you meant. Tell me what you did.

**Question Six: Which intelligence assessment, dated fourteen days before the attack, warned of 'coordinated multi-vector corridor breach' and was subsequently classified at the highest tier?**

This document exists. I have seen a reference to it in a deposition transcript. The deposition transcript is now sealed. The Assembly member who unsealed it briefly is no longer serving. I will keep asking.

**Question Seven: Why has no independent forensic reconstruction of the breach timeline ever been permitted?**

The Assembly commissioned one firm. One. Orion Trust Security Consulting, which holds 900 million SGC in SDC infrastructure contracts. Contradictions don't exist. Check your premises.

---

I am not telling you the Proxima Strike was staged. I am telling you the official account contains structural impossibilities that nobody in authority will address, and that the entities which stood to benefit most — financially, politically, militarily — from that attack were the same entities that shaped the subsequent inquiry.

That is not a conspiracy theory. That is a checklist.

If the account is true, answer the questions. Prove me wrong. I will print the correction on the front page.

I've been waiting three years. The silence is the story.
